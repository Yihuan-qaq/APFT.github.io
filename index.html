APFT: Adaptive Phoneme Filter Template to Generate Anti-compression Speech Adversarial Example in Real-time

Yihuan Huang2, Yanzhen Ren1,2, Member, IEEE, Zongkun Sun2, Liming Zhai3, Jingmin Wang2, Wuyang Liu2, Graduate Student Member, IEEE
1Key Laboratory of Aerospace Information Security and Trusted Computing, Ministry of Education 2School of Cyber Science and Engineering, Wuhan University 3School of Computer Science, Central China Normal University

Abstract

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) systems are extensively employed for speech censoring. Speech Adversarial Example (AE) offers a novel approach to protect speech privacy by forcing ASR to mistranscribe. However, existing speech AE face two challenges in real-time voice interactive applications, such as IP telephone, voice chat, or video conference, it cannot be generated in real-time, and its defensive capability is significantly reduced after the essential audio compression for network transmission.

In this paper, we propose the Adaptive Phoneme Filter Template (APFT) to address these issues. The key features of APFT introduces: 1) Phoneme-level Templates for universal AE generation in real-time, 2) Filter, which eliminates redundant signals to enhance compression robustness. 3) Adaptive Band Filtering, which limits the attack area from the frequency band without affecting the attack effectiveness and improves the speech quality.

The comprehensive experimental results show that APFT has four advantages: 1) Real-time Generation, with AE generation time below 1.1 ms for 1-second speech; 2) Compression Robustness, achieving a WER of 0.64 under AAC and Opus codecs; 3) Transferability, with an average WER of 0.72 across datasets and ASR systems; 4) Stealthiness, achieving a MOS of 4.07 for high-quality speech. In addition, the experiment in Telegram voice calls further proves the practical applicability of APFT.
